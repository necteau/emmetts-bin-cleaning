# DESIGN.md — Emmett’s Mobile Bin Cleaning Service

## Product personality
Young local operator, surprisingly professional, fast, clean, neighborly. The brand should feel fresher than the category: part pressure-wash grit, part spotless curb appeal.

## Audience / use context
Busy homeowners, parents, HOAs, small businesses, restaurants, and property managers who hate bin smell but do not want to think about it.

## Inspiration references
- Bright Bins: best overall model for clear problem framing, simple plans, and playful memorable tone.
- Bubble Binz/Wash Bins: useful “how it works” sequence and technical proof: steam/pressure/sanitize/deodorize.
- Epic Bin Cleaning: good offer-led CTA pattern.
- Refero direction: avoid default SaaS cards; use chunky editorial type, angled panels, and motion-based product storytelling.

## Visual direction
- Mood: wet, fresh, local, energetic, a little heroic for a humble service.
- Layout: strong hero with layered 3D bin/truck elements, before/after proof, diagonal process cards, sticky CTA/pricing.
- Color: clean aqua/blue, sanitation green, deep navy, safety-lime accents, warm cream background.
- Typography: oversized condensed-style display feel via system sans, tight tracking, bold utility labels.
- Components: floating clean-stamp badges, 3D bin cards, water bubbles, scroll-reveal sections, sticky quote panel.
- Motion/interactions: parallax hero mist, reveal-on-scroll cards, 3D tilt hover images, animated cleaning path.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
No generic pressure-washing template. No stolen competitor copy/images. No vague “quality service” sludge. No endless fake testimonials.

## Mobile behavior
Hero becomes stacked; sticky CTA remains reachable; pricing cards scroll naturally; animations reduce if motion disabled.

## Acceptance checklist
- Clear what service is in first 5 seconds.
- Shows problem → process → plan → booking path.
- Uses original generated imagery and original copy.
- Feels good enough to send as a real preview link.
